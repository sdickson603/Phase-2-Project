Tim Wakefield

Knuckleball Pitcher

A look at Tim Wakefield's life:

  • 1966 - Born in Melbourne, Florida
  • 1988 - Drafted in the eight round of by the Pittsburg Pirates
  • 1989 - Began developing a knuckleball and worked on becoming a pitcher
  • 1990 - Began as a full time pitcher in Single-A
  • 1991 - Advanced to Double-A and then to Triple-A
  • 1992 - Called up to the majors with the Pirates and threw a complete game
  • 1995 - After being released by the Pirates, he was signed by the Boston Red Sox
  • 1999-2008 - Pitched for the Red Sox in various relief roles, including closer, a fill in starter and then a permanent member of the rotation
  • 2009 - At 42, became the oldest Red Sox pitcher to pitch a complete game in his 15th season with the team
  • 2010 - Received the Roberto Clemente Award
  • 2011 - Season was followed in the documentary film "Knuckleball"
  • 2012 - Became a studio analyst for the Red Sox
  • 2023 - Wakefield died in his home at the age of 57 as a result of brain cancer.
